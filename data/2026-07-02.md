### [1] [Persona Without Substrate: Regime-Dependence and the LLM Individuation Problem](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00006)
*Shuaizhi Cheng*

Main category: cs.CL

TL;DR: The paper challenges a key assumption in an ontological framework for individuating LLM representations and proposes a regime-sensitive alternative, supported by empirical persona-topology experiments.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Beckmann & Butlin’s framework for solving the LLM individuation problem relies on an unexamined assumption from persona-vector work: that representational directions are stable and co-refer across different operational regimes (prompting, fine-tuning, and inference-time steering). The authors aim to test and problematize this assumption because it underpins several recent philosophical interpretations of LLM internal structure.

Method: They run persona-topology experiments on Qwen3-4B-Instruct and Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.2, comparing how persona-related representational directions behave under three regimes: (1) prompt-based extraction of persona vectors, (2) gradient-descent fine-tuning, and (3) inference-time activation steering. They analyze geometric relationships (e.g., collinearity) between prompt-extracted vectors and fine-tuning basins, study how fictional versus real personas move the model along these directions, investigate mixtures of contradictory personas, and contrast compositional behavior of persona algebra across regimes.

Result: They find: (1) prompt-extracted persona vectors are not collinear with the basins defined by fine-tuning, (2) fictional personas can shift model behavior further along directions anchored in real personas than those real anchors themselves, (3) mixtures of opposing-valence personas converge toward an attractor determined by the model’s training history rather than a neutral midpoint, and (4) compositional algebra over personas behaves asymmetrically when done via inference-time arithmetic compared to “chimera” fine-tuning, indicating regime-dependent representational structure.

Conclusion: The assumption that a single representational direction has the same content across prompting, fine-tuning, and steering is empirically unsupported. The authors propose a regime-indexed individuation principle: the basic unit for representational content is a (vehicle, regime) pair. Under this view, Beckmann & Butlin’s three proposed individuation options actually pick out different regime-internal items rather than genuine rivals for one and the same representational entity, and the same reinterpretation applies to other philosophical accounts (Mollo & Millière, Chalmers, Cerullo).

Abstract: Beckmann & Butlin's (2026) ontological framework for the LLM individuation problem inherits an unargued cross-regime co-reference assumption from the persona-vectors literature: that the same direction picks out the same content under prompt-conditioning, gradient-descent fine-tuning, and inference-time steering. We present four empirical wedges from persona-topology experiments on Qwen3-4B-Instruct and Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.2 - non-collinearity of prompt-extracted vectors and fine-tune basins; fictional personas displacing the model along real-anchor directions more strongly than real anchors do; contradictory-valenced mixtures biased toward a training-history-determined attractor; and asymmetric compositional algebra under inference-time arithmetic versus fine-tune-time chimera training - that jointly undermine the assumption. We propose regime-indexed individuation: the identity unit for representational content is a (vehicle, regime) pair, not a vehicle alone. Under this framework, Beckmann & Butlin's three candidate positions describe three different regime-internal objects rather than competing for the same referent; the same diagnosis applies to Mollo & Millière, Chalmers, and Cerullo.
