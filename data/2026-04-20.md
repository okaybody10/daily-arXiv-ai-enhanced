*Venkata Abhinandan Kancharla*

Main category: cs.CL

TL;DR: The paper empirically compares three explainability methods (Integrated Gradients, Attention Rollout, SHAP) on a DistilBERT model for SST-2 sentiment classification, highlighting stability–efficiency–flexibility trade-offs rather than proposing new techniques.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: LLMs and transformer models achieve strong accuracy but are hard to interpret, which undermines trust, debugging, and safe deployment. Method: Fine-tune a DistilBERT model on the SST-2 sentiment classification task, then apply three existing explainability techniques—Integrated Gradients (gradient-based attribution), Attention Rollout (attention-based), and SHAP (model-agnostic)—within a unified, reproducible evaluation setup. Conclusion: No single explainability method is universally best; each carries trade-offs among stability, interpretability, computational efficiency, and flexibility. The study offers practical guidance for choosing explainability tools for transformer-based NLP models like DistilBERT in real-world applications.

Rather than proposing new methods, the focus is on evaluating the practical behavior of existing approaches under a consistent and reproducible setup.

Model-agnostic approaches offer flexibility but introduce higher computational cost and variability.
